General FAQ

What is the delivery time of your wireless products?

We do not stock products but we can produce most products within a few weeks. Our core products are produced in batches; therefore products are only available every 2-3 months. Customers should confirm their order 1 month before production to make sure their order is included. Check the document ‘order deadlines’ about our production schedule and the deadline for orders.

What is the delivery time of your wireless products?

We do not stock products but we can produce most products within a few weeks. Our core products are produced in batches; therefore products are only available every 2-3 months. Customers should confirm their order 1 month before production to make sure their order is included. Check the document ‘order deadlines’ about our production schedule and the deadline for orders.

How can I sample or check the Astera lights?

We have at least 3 exhibitions per year where you can see demonstrations of our lights. We have showrooms in Germany and the US, and you can also rent our lights from the many clients we have worldwide.

Where can I get my Astera light repaired?

We have Service Centers located in Germany, the US, and China.

What kind of warranty do you offer?

Free replacement of damaged goods within 12 months. For batteries the warranty is only 3 months since a customer can harm the battery through incorrect storage.

Are your lights compatible with W-DMX lights?

W-DMX converters are very expensive so we use our own standard. When sending from a DMX console you can connect a W-DMX transmitter and also Astera’s wireless DMX transmitter you can send both signals at the same time.

Do you have CE, FCC, and UL approval?

Astera has CE certificates and our power supplies are FCC and UL approved.

Can you develop a customized product for me?

Astera has a background in product development and is currently producing lights for other companies. Talk to us about your project and we can tell you the price and MOQ (minimum order quantity) for your proposal.

Are the AX3, AX7 and AX10 available in a white housing?

Yes, we do white housings on request for larger orders at an added cost.

How many lights can I control with your remote controllers?

Providing the lights are in range of 300m, it is possible to control an unlimited amount of Astera lights with the ARC2 or AsteraTouch™ controllers. With the ARC1 Infra-red controller, the range is 10m.

What is the difference between a group and a set?

They are both features to assign effects to your lights. A group has the advantage of targeting several groups at the same time, but the disadvantage is that there are only four groups to allocate lights to. Sets have the advantage that there are 256 available spaces to make and store a set. You can assign positions and effects to lights within a set, but the disadvantage is that it is only possible to target one set at a time.

A set is your basic method of linking lights to perform effects. For basic users, this is all you will need to use. For advanced users, a combination of sets and groups are extremely useful. Groups are a quick way to link certain lights to perform the your chosen effects.

What is a chain?

Chains are a quick way to link two or more sets together so that effects can stretch through several sets, or through a combination of sets and individual lights. By using chains, it is possible to have effects that are stretched up to 1024 pixels.

Technical

What is Kelvin and what does it mean for lighting?

Kelvin is the unit of measurement given to color temperatures. The higher the temperature, the colder the color appears to the human eye. Temperatures over 5,000°K are known as cool colors, with lower colors around 3,000°K known as warm colors.

How accurate is the color calibration of Astera’s X-Series?

The difference between batches of new Astera lights is not noticeable to the human eye. Astera can match a Lee filter more accurate than any other light in this price segment. In numbers, Astera’s X-series can display warm white light with an accuracy of within 100°K.

Does Astera produce the color amber?

Astera can already display orange colors by mixing different amounts red and yellow. This looks orange to the human eye, but in fact this is not a true orange. Which can be seen when shone onto a brown/orange surface. Adding amber LEDs to the current RGBW LEDs will fix this and this can be seen in our X-Series range (AX7 and AX10 models).

Which radio band do you operate on?

Astera’s wireless lights circle the 868MHz band (~915MHz for the US).

How many lights can I control with one controller?

An unlimited amount of Astera lights can be controlled so long as they are in range. However, it is only possible to control 1,024 pixels at any one time.

Do the ARC2 and AsteraTouch™ controllers have a constant link with the lights?

No. The remote controllers only send for about 30 seconds. After this time, the internal processor memorizes the commands, and they will continue to play effects.

How many channels control your lights?

Astera’s RGBW LEDs are automatically calculated out of RGB values, so 3 channels are enough to control an Astera light. In addition, you can set 1 strobe channel for each or all pixels. Most Astera lights have multiple pixels but to save bandwidth you can set them to 1 or several pixels.

What does ‘pixel’ mean?

The term ‘pixel’ refers to each individually controllable element of a light. For example, the Wallwasher range is divided into segments (each segment is referred to as a pixel; Wallwasher small has 6, medium has 12, large has 24) whereas the Lightdrop™ can only display 1 pixel.

Can I use your wireless lights for TV productions or high-end video recording without them flickering?

Yes. Astera fixtures display light with a pseudo-random flicker which does not get recorded by video cameras. Check our website for a video display.

What benefit does the Amber LEDs give me?

Adding the amber light allows more accurate color reflection on yellow/brown/orange surfaces. With a standard yellow light shone onto an orange surface, the surface would be darkened, but with our new system, the surface will reflect more accurately the color being shone.

Can the AsteraTouch™ control competitor’s lights or just Astera lights?

The AsteraApp™ can only control Astera lights. However, you can use an ART3, set it to ‘receiver mode’ and connect it to other lights. Then, it is possible to control third-party lights from the AsteraApp™ or ARC2 controllers.

How many programs can I create using the AsteraTouch™?

The AsteraApp™ has 24 predefined programs, of which all are customizable. It has near unlimited pages (9 program slots per page) to create and save programs to.

Battery

What kind of warranty do you offer?

Free replacement of damaged goods within 12 months. For batteries the warranty is only 3 months since a customer can harm the battery through incorrect storage.

Are the batteries safe?

We are using high-grade lithium-nickel-cobalt-manganese batteries, similar to the ones used in laptops. As long as a battery does not have physical damage it is entirely safe.

How long does a battery last?

A battery lasts about 300 charging cycles, that means if you completely drain the battery and then fully charge them 300 times they will be at 70% capacity. If they are only partially drained when charging them you will get many more cycles than that.

How long do your lights run on a single charge?

All battery-powered fixtures are designed to display white light for 8 hours on normal brightness. Other colors such as red and green can be displayed twice as long.

How long does it take to charge your lights?

This differs slightly from light to light, but it should be around 6-8 hours for a completely drained battery.

What is the best way to store the batteries?

Always give them a full charge before storing them in your warehouse. A battery that is completely drained will slowly consume itself after 1 month, even less in extremely cold conditions. If stored in a warehouse, give them a charge every month to avoid damage.

What kind of batteries do you use?

Astera uses multi-element batteries that contain Lithium, Manganese, Nickel and Cobalt. This technology is superior to just Lithium-Ion batteries in terms of safety and battery lifetime, and are far superior to lead acid batteries which are bulky and heavy.

Can we replace the batteries if they break or wear out?

We offer battery replacement at official Astera service centers. Since the battery is the most expensive component of this product the cost is about 1/3 of a new fixture. If the product is covered by warranty, the replacement is free.

How often do we need to charge the batteries if unused?

When lights are not in use, keep fully charged. Re-charge monthly to avoid damage to the battery. Check before charging to see if the battery has drained. As all of our lights have different energy retention, check before recharging to see if it has power.

How long can I keep lights on standby (blackout) before they run out of battery?

It is possible to set up your lights, put them in blackout and leave them like this for over a week, and the loss of battery will be almost unnoticeable.

How long does the AsteraTouch™ run for?

Due to the radio frequency ability of the AsteraTouch™, it is constantly transmitting and therefore the battery is drained fairly quickly. Either switch it off when not in use, or keep it on charge (much like you might do with a mobile/cell phone).

How many AX7/AX10 can be daisy-chained for charging?

The AC cable ad Powercon True1 connectors are designed to charge up to 8 AX10s in a daisy chain, and up to 16 AX7s in a daisy chain.

DMX

Can I use my DMX controller with your lights?

It is possible to use pretty much any DMX controller. Simply connect the Astera ART3 and send wireless DMX commands to our wireless lights.

Can I assign a DMX address to your lights?

Some of our lights have buttons and a 3-line display which makes DMX settings easy. Smaller lights that do not have a display can receive DMX addresses sent by the ARC2 controller.

I already have some DMX fixtures; can I use them with your system?

You can connect up to 32 conventional DMX lights to one of our ART3 radio transmitters so they can receive wireless data from another ART3 or Astera remote control. This works with all DMX fixtures that can be set to 3 channel DMX modes (1 channel for each R, G and B) or 6 channel modes where 1 channel is each used for Red, Green, Blue and 3 other channels to be left at zero value.

Can your AsteraTouch™ transmit 1 DMX universe?

The AsteraTouch™ can transmit a complete DMX universe (512 channels), but if you change more than 100 channels quickly at the same time, the refresh rate will be slow and at times some of the lights might delay changing for 1-2 seconds. Switching the AsteraTouch™ into the RC transmitter mode can overcome this problem and can virtually control an unlimited amount of wireless Astera lights.

What is the difference between the protocols; DMX, Astera250 and Astera500?

DMX512, Astera250 and Astera500 are all data protocols to control LED lights. DMX is a digital data transmission universe for controllers and light equipment, and it is referred to as DMX512, which has cross-manufacturer compatibility. However, DMX512 is a slightly outdated standard protocol used by most companies throughout the world. Astera lights and controllers support DMX512, but we have also created our own protocols.

Astera250 is a much more stable protocol and has a higher refresh-rate then DMX512. Astera500 can control more pixels; one universe contains 250 pixels while a DMX universe only contains 170 pixels.

Can I run multiple universes of DMX on different frequencies with multiple ART3s?

It is possible to run DMX channels from different universes on up to 4 ART3s. Simply set the ART3s and lights to different radio pins. In theory, you can set up to 9999 different radio pins, and you cannot control more than 400-500 DMX channels as the bandwidth is not enough.

Do Astera lights support RDM?

Currently, Astera’s lights do not support DMX-RDM, but we are working on a feature so that lights can talk-back to their controllers.

What wireless DMX options are available?

All lights following the SpotMax™ (AX10), will be fitted with the ability to be controlled by CRMX Wireless DMX (from Lumen Radio), W-DMX (from Wireless Solutions) and by wireless DMX (via Astera’s ART3 Wireless DMX Transmitter).

How do I unpair my lights with CRMX Wireless DMX?

On the AX3, press the ON/OFF button for approx. 5 secs until it turns red, then it has been factory reset, which wipes the connection to the Lumen Radio transmitter. On the AX10 and AX7, navigate yourself to the DMX SETTINGS menu and choose UNPAIR CRMX.

LED

What is an LED and how does it work?

An LED is a Light Emitting Diode. An LED works by a process called electroluminescence. Inside an LED, there are two regions, a P region (with positively charged holes) and an N region (with negative electrons). Where these regions meet is called the PN junction. When enough voltage is passed through the device, negatively charged electrons can eventually pass the junction and meet with positively charged holes. Their combination releases electromagnetic energy in the form of a photon of light.

How long do your LEDs last?

After approximately 50,000 hours of operation, our LEDs will be at 70% brightness. This translates into 5 years and 8 months of constant use.

Does an LED overheat?

LEDs do get hot, and the hotter an LED becomes, the shorter its life expectancy is. LEDs are insufficient to control their own temperature. Therefore Astera has revolutionized the housing of its fixtures to include extensive heat sinks which dissipate the heat into the surrounding air, keeping the LEDs cool, and allowing them to last much longer than other lighting manufacturers. Furthermore, Astera’s X-Series boasts heat-conductive plastic, making our lights weigh less but with the same operational efficiency and cooling ability of our previous range.

What is Luminous Emittance?

Luminous emittance is quantitative term for brightness, and shows the total luminous flux shone onto a surface (per unit area). Emittance is measured in Lux (or Lumens) per square meter.

How bright are Astera’s lights?

The important thing to note when looking at brightness of LEDs is that total LED power is not the sole factor to consider. Luminous efficacy is also very important as it measures how well a light fixture produces visible light. Astera produces high power LEDs (up to 160W), meaning the total power consumed by the LEDs is very high, but it also offers high luminous efficacy due to using high-quality Cree LEDs. Astera produces the brightest wireless lights for their size. For example, the Lightdrop™, a small and versatile 90mm spotlight, has a total LED power of 10W and a luminous efficacy of 70 Lumens/Watt, which is very bright. Many brands offer high power, but low luminous efficacy.

What is maximum operating temperature for an LED?

The ambient operating temperature of our wireless lights is up to 40°C. The storage and transportation temperature of our lights is substantially higher, around double the temperature.

What is the CRI for Astera lights?

The exact CRI for our lights varies depending on the color temperature chosen, but on cold white, our lights can achieve an average of 87Ra.
