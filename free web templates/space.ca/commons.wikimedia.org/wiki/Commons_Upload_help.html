How to upload files to Commons

Anyone, from newbie to experienced, can ask a question here. Questions will be replied to here as well. Any answers you receive are not legal advice and the responder cannot be held liable for them. Please sign your question by typing four tildes (~~~~). In order to get quick answers consider the following points:

  • For help regarding the Wiki Loves Monuments 2014 contest, please use the WLM help desk instead.
  • For help needed on Commons issues other than uploading, please see the main help desk and links from there.
  • For questions not related to this website, try the Wikipedia reference desk.
  • Have you checked the FAQ and help contents?
  • You may also find what you are looking for in the archives.
  • Resolved sections (marked by {{section resolved|1=~~~~}}) will be archived after four days. Sections with no discussion will be archived after ten days.


Help us to help you: click here for guidance
 Help us to help you:
  • Copyright: If your question is about copyright, please provide as much information as possible about (i) the source and author of the digital file you are uploading (ii) the source and author of any creative works shown in the digital file (eg for a photograph of a sculpture, who the sculptor is). The year and country of publication and the year of death of the author are often needed to show that a work is in the public domain. The more information you can provide, the faster and more clearly the volunteers here will be able to answer your question.
  • Identifiable people: If your question is about the policy on photographs of identifiable people, please make sure you have read Commons:Photographs of identifiable people (the Commons:Photographs of identifiable people/checklist can help you apply the policy). The country in which the photograph was taken is often essential information here.


  • If you have already uploaded the file(s) you are asking about, please provide a wikilink, in this form: [[:File:filename]].
  • If the file is available online, please provide a link to it, in this form: [http://www.example.com/pagewherefilecanbeseen.html].

Choosing a license for an SVG recreation of a flag[edit]

I created an SVG image of this flag and want to upload it. I used the original file (which appears to be from vector-images.com) as a visual aid to create the SVG.

  1. Do I have to credit vector-images.com as an/the author?
  2. Can I choose an attribution-type license, or must the SVG be public domain, since the original doesn't appear to be copyrighted as per Russian law?

The discussion on the subject doesn't really help me, the only thing I have understood is that it seems to be complicated :-). --Rwchhs (talk) 19:52, 29 August 2014 (UTC)

As far as I can see, only the original small file uploaded in 2007 is sourced to the Vector-Images site, while the basic underlying flag design is uncopyrightable under Russian law. So if you ignore the small 2007 image when making your vector, then you don't have to mention Vector-Images at all... AnonMoos (talk) 20:08, 30 August 2014 (UTC)

help with photo file[edit]

Hello, Having trouble, and will appreciate your help. I used the wizard to upload a photo file, but instead of landing within the page, it went into commons, kind of like a separate page. How do I get it into the wiki page? -- 15:33, 2 September 2014 User:Francoisg

First you have to upload the image, then add the image to the article or gallery page. If the name of the uploaded image is "Sample.jpg", then edit the page where you want to add the image to include [[File:Sample.jpg|thumb|"Image description or caption goes here"]] or similar... AnonMoos (talk) 16:57, 2 September 2014 (UTC)

Cannot upload Image[edit]

I am trying to upload a graph from Wikimedia commons into a Wikipedia article. When I try to upload the Image I get the following error message: You do not have permission to upload this file, for the following reason: The action you have requested is limited to users in one of the groups: Autoconfirmed users, Administrators, Confirmed users.

Please explain --Aakol1234 (talk) 20:06, 5 September 2014 (UTC)

Usually this means that you're trying to overwrite an existing file, and your account has been registered for less than 4 days... AnonMoos (talk) 23:10, 5 September 2014 (UTC)
@Aakol1234: if the image is already on Commons, you shouldn’t be trying to upload it to WP: instead, just edit the article to add a File template with the link and it will display there.
