This help desk is a forum for questions and help on

How to use Commons

Anyone, from newbie to experienced, can ask a question here. Questions will be replied to here as well. Any answers you receive are not legal advice and the responder cannot be held liable for them. Please sign your question by typing four tildes (~~~~). In order to get quick answers consider the following points:

  • For licensing and copyright issues, post at Commons:Village pump/Copyright.
  • For changing the name of a file see Commons:File renaming.
  • For proposals, post at Commons:Village pump/Proposals.
  • For policy and technical information, post at Commons:Village pump.
  • For administrator attention, post here, Commons:Administrators' noticeboard or directly to an administrator.
  • For questions not related to this website, try Wikipedia:Reference desk.
  • Have you checked the Commons:FAQ and Help:Contents?
  • You may also find what you are looking for in the Commons:Help desk/Archives.
  • Resolved sections (marked by {{section resolved|1=~~~~}}) will be archived after four days, sections with no discussion will be archived after ten days.

EthnicRussiansInTheFormerUSSR.png Licensing and originality[edit]

The map File:EthnicRussiansInTheFormerUSSR.png is a derivative work of a map called the QBAM, maintained at the site AlternateHistory.com as a community project. Our strong preference is that the AH.com user Qazaq and the name of the map be referenced, and that mention be made that it is maintained at AH.com, as it is the product of several years of work there. The current file information indicates that the user has created the map themselves, and this is not the case. Xt828 (talk) 01:48, 31 August 2014 (UTC)

When you say "our strong preference", who is we here? It would be nice if you linked to the page on AlternateHistory.com (not AH.com!) that has this image on it. — Preceding unsigned comment added by Prosfilaes (talk • contribs) 05:37, 31 August 2014‎ (UTC)
It would make things much easier if Xt828 would provide a direct link to the map he believes is the map from which File:EthnicRussiansInTheFormerUSSR.png is immediately derived. From the clues above, I've spent much time trying to find the source map on that website, which by the way is a discussion forum, fighting with the captcha of its search tool on every search, getting links to threads with cryptic titles and hundreds of posts. Searching user "Qazaq" returns "invalid user", as apparently there's no user there with that name. But a thread eventually uncovered a user named "Qazaq2007". Still not finding the elusive source map after reading through dozens of his posts. Also, it seems that user ceased his contributions there in 2010. The closest-looking maps I could find were after going through links by other users to other websites, for example there and there. There's certainly a strong resemblance with File:EthnicRussiansInTheFormerUSSR.png, although there are also differences. It is plausible that they may be derived from a common source map. Of the two maps linked above, the one named "old_world_2008_subdivisions.png" is credited to Qazaq2007 by wiki.alternatehistory.com. There doesn't seem to be any indication there that it might have been offered under a free license by that person. However, that map, apparently from 2008, looks like it is based on the map File:A large blank world map with oceans marked in blue.PNG, which was started on the Commons in 2005 (and is not in the public domain but under licenses with share-alike obligations for derivatives), with the addition of the internal subdivisions. So, not sure who copied who. The original map may have been copied from Commons by the user of alternatehistory.com without crediting the original and without the license, and then the map modified with the subdivisions copied the other way around. The link to the map file "CRGNrZQ.png" was from the bottom of this post and is not credited there to an author's name. However, the larger context of the discussions in the threads there, in light of the presentation above by Xt828, may allow the conclusion that Qazaq2007 seems considered the author of a version of that map. There doesn't seem to be any indication that this map is offered under a free license by its author(s) either. That means, unfortunately, that Xt828, either personally or as an official representative of the website alternatehistory.com (I guess that's what he meant by "our"), can't give us a permission to use that map under a free license, without an explicit confirmation somewhere that the author(s) themselves have offered their work under a free license. If that map is derived from the others, including the 2005 map, the 2008 map, etc., then the contributing authors in the series should be credited and the license situation clarified. The uploader of File:EthnicRussiansInTheFormerUSSR.png should be invited to comment. -- Asclepias (talk) 12:41, 31 August 2014 (UTC)

Actually, as the person who made this map, I am pretty sure that this map is a derivative work of this map -- http://darussophile.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/natural-population-growth-ussr-2012.png

To fix the informations, you can edit the description page of the file, mention and link to all previous works that you have used, distinguish what is your work from what is the work of other people, attribute each part of the work to its source and actual author, respect the licensing status of the works. In this case, if the map you linked above was the map from which you derived your map, that only displaces the problem. There does not seem to be a free license associated to that map on that website. You can't offer a work under a CC-0 declaration without the consent of all the authors of the works from which it is derived. Also, that website doesn't seem to mention from where it copied that map. The original poster in this section seemed to notice an uncredited filiation from, or at least a common origin with, another map. And that other map itself may have an uncredited filiation, or at least a common origin with, one or several previous map(s) from Commons. -- Asclepias (talk) 21:37, 31 August 2014 (UTC)

Help with uploading picture. Yep I tried.[edit]

Real simple issue : I have 77 year old image, of my 2nd Great Grand Uncle, that was passed down with our family estate. As his Wikipedia page is missing an image of him, I would like to have the image I have be placed on his page.

Wikipedia Page: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edmund_Noble

Image: https://drive.google.com/file/d/0B5XcZM38cLA3U0drcWN1N1M2aDA/edit?usp=sharing

I tried to follow the steps but it seems I dont have enough edits to gain the rights to upload images.

Thanks for any assistance anyone can provide. — Preceding unsigned comment added by Cjaysnyder (talk • contribs) 19:40, 31 August 2014‎ (UTC)

Hello CJ. At Commons we need to know about the copyright status of every image, including who took the photo, where they took it, whether it was published or not, roughly when it was taken and if it was a long time ago we need to know who holds the rights to the photo now. The best route is for the copyright owner of the photo (usually but not always the photographer) to send an email to permissions-commons@wikimedia.org using the format at COM:ET. The email system is operated by a team of volunteers who will get in touch with the emailer and advise them of what needs to be done. Green Giant (talk) 20:45, 31 August 2014 (UTC)
As above, but if you can't identify the rights-holder, and there are no other free pictures, then you can probably upload the picture to Wikipedia as "fair use". We can't accept fair use images here at Commons, because all our media must be free to use for any purpose.
Photos of deceased persons are usually acceptable as fair use due to the impossibility of taking another picture. See for example the image used to illustrate en:Isaac Shoenberg. You can ask for help at Wikipedia if you get stuck.
Incidentally, we do have some media related to Edmund Noble at Category:Edmund Noble. No pictures of him, only postcards he sent :) –⁠moogsi (talk) 05:43, 1 September 2014 (UTC)
We would still need to establish whether it was published for fair use on enwiki, as previously unpublished images can't be used per w:WP:NFCC#4. January (talk) 06:44, 1 September 2014 (UTC)

Graphics display question[edit]

Hello. I am not sure if this is the best place to place this question. In case it is not could I be suggested the appropriate place? But anyways, my question is... how does one find out the proper graphics display of images here? Basically, what I am getting at is that I see certain images differently depending on which monitor I am using. It sounds odd, and to me it is odd, but I am just wondering if there is a way to find out what is the supposed "standard"? (Lilic (talk) 02:00, 3 September 2014 (UTC)).

Hello Lilic. Yes this is the right place to ask this question! Basically there is no "standard" because users are able to set their own preferences. Click on your Preferences at the top of any Commons page when you are logged in. Then click on the Appearances tab and look for the Files section which should be the fourth one down. There will be two options, for image size limit and thumbnail size. Then I'd recommend that you open a separate tab or window with any image and change the options to see which ones feel right for you. Some people like to have everything at maximum, others at minimum and probably most people somewhere in the middle. Green Giant (talk) 08:28, 3 September 2014 (UTC)
Thanks for your reply. I didn't exactly mean that - I meant the monitor itself! Different monitors show some colors differently, depending if I am at home, at the university, a friends place, the library, or so forth... the way the color appears on screens shows up differently... (Lilic (talk) 21:56, 3 September 2014 (UTC)).
Ah well then that is something out of our hands because it depends entirely on the make of your monitors, which operating system is in use at home and elsewhere, and what the graphic settings are. Green Giant (talk) 22:23, 3 September 2014 (UTC)
Mm, okay, so just last question... there is no "standard" ? If so well okay then. :/ I must say though, some stuff that I uploaded looks quite odd on "some" monitors, while quite fine on others... the yellow color hues, in particular. (Lilic (talk) 04:01, 4 September 2014 (UTC)).
Computer monitors can vary quite widely on how accurately they can display colors and incorrect settings will ruin the colors on even the most perfect monitor. You would need to have a fairly good quality monitor color calibrated with a colorimeter for a start. Then you must remember that Commons hosts images from many different photographers and cameras. Some photographs may have been taken with a simple point-and-shoot compact camera with automatic color balance while another is taken with a professional DSLR using custom color balance settings. Then there are professional archival TIFF files, images with embedded color profiles (which your browser / image viewer software may or may not understand)... showing "true" colors of an image is a tricky science with no easy answers. Uploading images from Flickr[edit]

I've only uploaded an image here once before - in that case, I had the copyright holder send me an e-mail, in which he explicitly stated which license he was releasing the photo under. I then forwarded the e-mail to someone who works here at the Commons.

Hi, Insert the the template Flickrreview in the description page of the file. Make sure you mention the link to the flickr page as the source. -- Asclepias (talk) 20:39, 4 September 2014 (UTC)
Hi Jpcase. As Asclepias states, the way to get round this problem is to use the {{flickrreview}} template. While people can stop distributing images under a free licence, they cannot be revoked. The flickrreview template asks a trusted user or bot to come along, look at the image on Commons and flickr, check they're the same and that it's on flickr under a free licence. That way, even if the flickr user changes the licence, we can say "it was released freely at this time". -mattbuck (Talk) 15:50, 6 September 2014 (UTC)

If the FBI has made a verifiable statement that they own the copyright, then the photograph is public domain. The template to use is {{PD-USGov-FBI}}.

  • Yes, obviously you are finding this confusing. For starters, you are not asking this question on Wikipedia. This is the Wikimedia Commons. Like the many language-specific Wikipedias, the Commons is a operated by the Wikimedia Foundation (WMF).
